App for aesthetic longevity intelligence:
* detects how old the face looks today
* estimates persistent face-age drift
* estimates rate of ageing ++ a rate of recovery/reversal

4. AGE MODEL 
4.1 Chronological Age (CA)
* user-entered date of birth
* fixed anchor
* used only as reference baseline and delta comparator
* not used to force daily score movement
________________


4.2 FaceAge Display (FAD)
What the face appears like today, decomposed:
* FAD = SFA + RAB
Where:
* SFA = Structural FaceAge (slow, deconfounded)
* RAB = Reversible Ageing Burden (daily/weekly burden that makes face look older)
________________


4.3 Structural FaceAge (SFA)
Slow-moving estimate from deconfounded structural/dynamic trends:
* persistent residual drift after subtracting short-term exposure effects
* stability- and quality-gated
* modified by resilience evidence support/contradiction
________________


4.4 Reversible Ageing Burden (RAB)
Short-term burden that inflates perceived age:
* puffiness load- 
* under-eye vitality drop
* redness/reactivity load
* dullness/luminosity loss
* face instability / volatility burden
Moves fast with:
* sleep/alcohol/sodium/stress/products/illness/travel
________________


4.5 Bio-Resilience Age Proxy (BRA) (product-defined biological proxy)
 cannot claim true clinical biological age without biomarkers. Product should compute a recovery biology proxy age from tissue behavior + recovery dynamics.
BRA Inputs
* press/release rebound features
* recovery speed after perturbations
* volatility burden
* recovery strain
* resilience trend stability
* contradiction-adjusted repeatability
Output
* Bio-Resilience Age Proxy
* BRA confidence
* BRA trend
* BRA vs Chronological Age delta
This gives a second age axis:
* Face looks older today (RAB high) but recovery biology stable (BRA stable)
* or face looks okay today but recovery biology worsening (early warning)
________________


4.6 Core Deltas (user-facing)
* FaceAge Display - Chronological Age
* Structural FaceAge - Chronological Age
* Bio-Resilience Age Proxy - Chronological Age
* FaceAge Display - Bio-Resilience Age Proxy (appearance burden gap)- not needed

5. RATE OF AGEING (IP)
5.1 Ageing Rate Is Not One Number
 computes three rates:
A. Apparence Velocity (fast)
How quickly visible age appearance is changing (reversible-heavy)
B. Structural Velocity (slow)
Residual slope of structural face-age component after deconfounding
C. Pressure-Adjusted Velocity Risk (forward risk)
Risk proxy that current habits/recovery/resilience will accelerate visible structural ageing if sustained
________________


5.2 FaceAge Velocity Decomposition
* FAV_display
* FAV_structural
* FAV_risk
User sees:
* What changed
* Why it changed
* Whether it is temporary
* What to do to slow/reverse
________________


5.3 Reversal Logic (visible + structural)
Reversible reversal
RAB drops due to sleep/alcohol/sodium/stress/product correction
Recovery reversal
Recovery strain drops + resilience improves + faster return-to-baseline
Structural reversal candidate
Residual structural slope improves over sufficient window and contamination low and press/resilience supports (or does not contradict)
No “reverse ageing” claims without thresholded evidence.

9.3 Layer 1 — Lagged Delta Correlation
Purpose
Identify short-lag drivers of daily state movement.
Lags
* T0
* T+1
* T+2
Workflow
1. select target state/vector
2. build exposure-present windows
3. build matched control windows
4. compare delta distributions per lag
5. measure sign consistency
6. measure effect vs personal noise band
7. apply capture quality weighting
8. apply confound and perturbation penalties
9. emit ranked hypotheses
Output
Per state:
* top driver list with lag, direction, effect band, confidence, confound risk
________________


9.4 Layer 2 — Cumulative Load Correlation
Purpose
Detect threshold/streak effects missed by single-event logic.
Modeled loads
* alcohol clustering
* sleep debt
* stress streaks
* repeated reactivity flares
* product consecutive use buildup
* recovery streak benefit
Workflow
1. compute rolling exposure load (2/3/5/7-day windows)
2. compare single-event vs streak effect
3. detect threshold points
4. convert hypothesis to conditional if threshold-dependent
5. update driver ranking and protocol suggestions
Output examples
* “Alcohol weak on single nights, strong after 3-night streak”
* “Sleep debt amplifies under-eye and dullness beyond night-1 effect”

9.7 Layer 5 — Interaction Effects (Conditional Ageing Logic)
Purpose
Model “X matters only when Y” or “X becomes worse under Y”.
Examples
* alcohol × late sleep → puffiness amplification
* sodium × cycle phase → puffiness sensitivity
* product × low sleep → redness/reactivity flare
* hydration × no alcohol → stronger luminosity effect
* stress × irregular sleep → face stability collapse
* improvement in appearance + declining resilience → false positive reversal risk
Gating
* base effects must mature first
* sufficient overlap samples
* stable sign
* confounds below threshold
Output
Conditional hypotheses and protocol suggestions.
________________


9.8 Layer 6 — Contradiction Handling (Retention + Accuracy)
Contradiction Types
* expected effect absent
* unexpected effect present
* competing exposures
* measurement uncertainty
* prediction miss
* appearance/resilience divergence
* adherence failure
System behavior
1. classify contradiction
2. downgrade/rerank hypotheses
3. log contradiction
4. generate next disambiguation experiment
5. trigger one adaptive prompt if needed
6. protect trust (no fake certainty)
Contradictions are treated as signal, not engine failure.
